Data Visualization for California Public Employee Compensation

Every year, public employers in California are required to report employee compensation data to the State Controller’s office. Websites like GCC (Government Compensation in California) and Transparent California have collected and cleaned this data so that it can be accessed for analysis.

Our goal at CPEC (California Public Employee Compensation) is to make this data more accessible to the public through a series of visualizations that anyone can explore to learn more about how public employees in their area are being compensated.

Our visualization begins on this page, with a map overview of average employee wages by city and county. Then, explore your city’s data by clicking on the “Explore your City” tab. This easy-to-use dashboard allows you to access a general overview of your city’s compensation data, break down the data by department, and compare your city’s data to up to 4 other cities.

Finally, if you are interested in learning more about compensation trends across the state, visit the “Data Room” where our in-house experts have done deep dives into specific topics that you may be interested in.

Thank you for visiting our page, and happy data exploration!
